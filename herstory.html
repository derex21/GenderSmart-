Herstory: Women's Role in Society

Welcome to our journey through women's history and their remarkable contributions to society. This page celebrates the achievements, struggles, and ongoing progress in gender equality.

Key Milestones in Women's History

1903

The First Woman in Politics

Hover to learn more

In 1903, the first woman was elected into the Philippine Congress, marking a milestone in women's political participation.

1941

Women's Right to Vote

Hover to learn more

In 1941, women in the Philippines gained the right to vote, a significant achievement in gender equality.

1995

Beijing Declaration

Hover to learn more

The 1995 Beijing Conference marked a global agreement on advancing gender equality and the empowerment of women.

Did You Know?

Marie Curie was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize in 1903 for her pioneering research on radioactivity. She is also the only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different sciences: Physics and Chemistry.
